History

The CHU was successfully established in 1990 by a group of influential entrepreneurs in the suburban region of Hsinchu County. With the main objective to inspire and facilitate quality education, promote the advancement of local industries and to foster regional prosperity has been the tenet for university’s success. It was formerly known as Chung Hua Polytechnic Institute before it was upgraded to its current status in 1997. At present, the University has six colleges, namely, the college of Information and Electrical Engineering, the college of Management, the college of Architecture and Design, the college of Humanities and Social Sciences, the college of Tourism and the college of Innovative Industries. As of 2019, the total strength of the students enrolled in the university is more than 5000 students and well supported by more than 220 teaching faculties across varies degree programs and colleges of CHU.
The COM was established in 1997 and it’s one of the largest academic units in the CHU. The COM offers four degree programs i.e. Bachelors of Business Administration BBA, Master of Science MS, Master of Business Administration MBA, & Ph.D. For the BBA Degree program there are five departments, including Department of Business Administration, Department of Finance, Department of Industrial Management, Department of Information Management, & Department of International Business. For the MS Degree program there are three departments, including Department of Business Administration, Department of Industrial Management, & Department of Information Management. For the MBA Degree program, it includes Department of Business Administration. And for the Ph.D. Degree program, it includes Department of Technology Department. A total of 7 bachelor’s degrees, 6 master’s degrees, & 1 doctoral degree are offered. There are around 64 participating faculty members and more than 1000 students in the college.
With the ambition to be distinguishable in the region, the COM has been continuously striving forward in achieving its mission under the outstanding leadership of its president, along with the joint efforts of all the faculty members including the staff, students and thoroughly supported by the Board of Directors has been the key to success for the COM in the region followed by investments in outreach and community partnerships.
